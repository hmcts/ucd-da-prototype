Upload documents Witness statements and evidence

If you are uploading documents from a computer, name the files clearly. For example, letter-from-school.doc.

Files must end with JPG, BMP, PNG,TIF, PDF, DOC or DOCX.

How to take a picture of a document on your phone and upload it
  1. Place your document on a flat service in a well-lit room. Use a flash if you need to.
  2. Take a picture of the whole document. You should be able to see its edges.
  3. Check you can read all the writing, including the handwriting.
  4. Email or send the photo or scan to the device you are using now.
  5. Upload it using the method below.
! Warning If there is information that must not be shared with the other people in the case, make sure it is not included in any documents you upload. If this is not possible you can ask the court to restrict who can see the document.
Rules about sharing emails, letters and documents:

When you upload documents, including emails and letters, these are automatically shared with the other people in the case. The only exceptions are if:

  1. there is a very good reason not to share the document, for example safety concerns.
  2. the document is not something the judge needs to see
  3. one of the people in the case has an address that needs to be kept private

If you believe a document you are uploading must not be shared with the other people in the case, you can ask the court to keep it private.
