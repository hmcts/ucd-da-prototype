Safety concerns

The court needs to know if anyone who spends time with the children poses a risk to their safety or yours.

Behaviours the court needs to know about

The court will need to know about abusive behaviour that you or the children have experienced.

The abuse could be:

  • physical
  • psychological
  • emotional
  • sexual
  • financial

If the other person in the case has abducted the children, this is also a form of abuse.

Why the court needs this information

If another person in the case is a risk to the safety of the children, the court needs to know so that it can handle your case correctly.

If you are not sure if the behaviour is a form of abuse

If you are not sure if the behaviour is abusive, see the guidance on types of abusive behaviour.

If you don't feel ready to describe the abuse at this stage

If you don't feel ready to talk about the abuse right now, you can do so when you speak to Cafcass.

Cafcass is an organisation that looks after the interests of children who are involved in family court cases.

How Cafcass can support you in your case

We will now ask you some questions about the abusive behaviour.

! Warning The information that you give in this section will also be shared with the respondent, so they are able to respond to what you have said.

Take your time filling in the information and write as much as you feel able to.
