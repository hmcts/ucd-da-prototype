Upload documents Witness statement and evidence

Consider when providing evidence:

  • if you are taking a picture of a letter, place it on a flat surface and take the picture from above
  • If you are uploading documents from a compute, name the files clearly. For example, 'letter-from-school.doc'
  • Files must end with JPG, BMP, PNG,TIF, PDF, DOC or DOCX
How to take a picture of a document on your phone and upload it
  1. Place your document on a flat service in a well-lit room. Use a flash if you need to.
  2. Take a picture of the whole document. You should be able to see its edges.
  3. Check you can read all the writing, including the handwriting.
  4. Email or send the photo or scan to the device you are using now.
  5. Upload it using the method below.
Rules about sharing emails, letters and documents:

You must copy any correspondence you send to the court to the other parties in the case. The only exceptions are::

  1. When there is a very good reason not to, for example safety concerns.
  2. The correspondence is purely administrative.
  3. A party has a confidential address. In these circumstances the court will forward the correspondence for you.
  4. When you are prevented by law from communicating with another party (for example by an injunction order) and that other party does not have a legal representative to whom you can send the correspondence.

You must clearly state in your correspondence that it has been copied to the other parties and give their names and their status in the proceedings or state the reason why it has not been copied. If you fail to do so the court will usually return your correspondence to you without taking any further action.

If you do not copy your correspondence to the other parties, you must say in your correspondence with the court which of the exceptions listed here you are relying on and why you consider it applies in your case.

! Warning If there is information you do not want to share with the other people in the case, make sure it is not included in any documents you upload. If this is not possible and you have to upload private information, you can ask the court to restrict who can see the document
